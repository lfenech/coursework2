Data Visualization

Data visualization is seen by many people as a modern source of visual communication. Data visualisation involves the study and implementation of the data in a visual state, this means information that has been abstracted in some schematic form, including attributes or variables for the units of information.
The first important thing that involvesDV is to communicate information clearly and efficiently with the user of statistical graphics, plots and information graphics. Numerical data may be encoded using dots, lines, or bars, to visually display a message. Effective visualization helps users understand and reason out data. Users may have particular analytical tasks, such as comparing or understanding causality, and the design of the graphiv which follows the task. Tables are mostly used in occassions where users search for a measurement. On the other hand charts are user to show patterns or relationships for 1 or more variables.
Data visualisation can be said to be and art or even a science. It is viewed by some as descriptive statistics or even as a grounded theory development tool. Data in the past years has increased. Big Data is the data created by internet activity and a number of sensors in the evvironment like satellites.
